Formulas & Assumptions

Core Equations

  • Velocity: V = Q / A where A = πD²/4
  • Reynolds Number: Re = ρVD/μ
  • Friction Factor (Laminar, Re < 2000): f = 64/Re
  • Friction Factor (Turbulent, smooth pipe): Haaland approximation
    1/√f = -1.8 log[(6.9/Re) + (k/D)/3.7¹.11]
    assuming hydraulically smooth hose (k ≈ 0.0015 mm)
  • Friction Pressure Drop: ΔPf = f · (L/D) · (ρV²/2)
  • Static Head: Hstatic = Δz (positive = lifting up)
  • TDH: Htotal = ΔPf/(ρg) + Δz
  • Hydraulic Power: Ph = ΔPtotal · Q
  • Shaft Power: Pshaft = Ph / η

Assumptions & Caveats

  • Steady, fully-developed flow in a circular hose — entrance effects neglected
  • Hydraulically smooth hose roughness assumed (k ≈ 0.0015 mm, typical food-grade hose)
  • Fluid treated as Newtonian — wine & juice behave as such at typical working conditions
  • No fittings, valves, or bends included — add ~10–30% friction allowance for typical winery piping
  • Pump efficiency is a user-supplied estimate; real pump curves vary with flow & head
  • Educational / first-pass only. Do not use for final pump selection or engineering certification
  • Consult a licensed engineer for design or safety-critical applications
