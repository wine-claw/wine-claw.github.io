📖 How it works ▼

This simulation uses a simplified Green-Ampt infiltration model. Here's what you're seeing:

  • Rain falls at the intensity you choose. Not all of it enters the soil.
  • Infiltration rate depends on the soil's saturated hydraulic conductivity (Ks) and how deep the wetting front has already penetrated.
  • Sand has huge pores and drains fast — water shoots deep with almost no runoff.
  • Clay has tiny pores and low conductivity — water can't get in quickly, so it ponds on the surface and runs off.
  • The wetting front marks the boundary between wet (field capacity) and dry soil. It moves slower as it goes deeper because the pressure gradient weakens.
  • Surface ponding appears when rainfall exceeds the soil's infiltration capacity for a sustained period.

Parameters like porosity, field capacity, wilting point, and suction head are based on typical USDA soil texture values. The model is explicit-timestep and simplified for visual clarity — not for engineering design.
