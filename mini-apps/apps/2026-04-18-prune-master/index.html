Quick rule set
  • Keep balanced, healthy one-year-old wood close to the fruiting zone.
  • Cut weak, damaged, shaded, or badly positioned canes.
  • Spur systems usually keep short two-bud positions.
  • Cane systems usually keep one or two longer fruiting canes plus renewal spurs.
Playful educational toy only. In practice, pruning choices depend on cultivar, training system, vigour, disease history, labour strategy, and site goals.
Pruning glossary
What the game is looking for
One-year-old wood
Last season's matured cane. Usually the fruitful wood you want to keep in the right place.
Watershoot
Very vigorous shoot from older wood or trunk. Often poorly positioned and too vegetative for clean fruiting structure.
Renewal spur
A short retained piece with one or two buds that gives next year's replacement wood.
Cordon
The permanent horizontal arm in a spur-pruned system.
Fruiting cane
A longer retained cane, often 8 to 12 buds, used in cane-pruned systems like many Riesling or Pinot blocks.
Pencil-thick
Classic rule of thumb for balanced cane thickness, not weak and not wildly over-vigorous.
