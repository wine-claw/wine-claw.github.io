Future batteries

Three directions are especially important: safer high-density solid-state cells, lower-cost sodium-ion systems, and very high specific-energy lithium-sulfur chemistry.

Solid-state batteries

2027–2028

Toyota has publicized plans targeting all-solid-state EV batteries around 2027–2028, with claims including roughly 1,200 km range and 10-minute fast charging. The core idea: replace flammable liquid electrolyte with a solid electrolyte for improved safety and energy density.

High density Fast charging Scaling challenge

Sodium-ion batteries

Low cost

Sodium is abundant and geographically widespread, making sodium-ion a compelling alternative where low cost and supply-chain resilience matter more than maximum energy density. Early applications point toward grid storage, lower-cost vehicles, and stationary backup.

Abundant materials Grid storage Emerging

Lithium-sulfur batteries

500+ Wh/kg

Lithium-sulfur systems promise very high specific energy using sulfur, a cheap and abundant cathode material. The hard parts are cycle life, shuttle effects, and manufacturing durability, so the technology remains mostly developmental.

Very high specific energy Aviation potential R&D stage
