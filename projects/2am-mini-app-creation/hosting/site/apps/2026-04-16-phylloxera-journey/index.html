The Rootstock Revolution

When phylloxera swept through Europe, conventional wisdom said the wine world was doomed. Then scientists and growers discovered a countermeasure hidden in nature itself: grafting European vines (Vitis vinifera) onto the roots of American species (Vitis labrusca, Vitis riparia, Vitis rupestris) that had evolved resistance to phylloxera. The graft union became viticulture's salvation — European fruit on American resilience.
