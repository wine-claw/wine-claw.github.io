How to Use the COP Calculator

What is Cost of Production?

Cost of Production (COP) measures the total cost required to produce one kilogram of liveweight or dressed weight of meat. It's the key metric for understanding your livestock enterprise's profitability and comparing performance against industry benchmarks.

The COP Formula

COP ($/kg) = Total Costs ÷ Total kg Produced

Where Total Costs include:

  • Direct expenses (health, contractors, transport, selling)
  • Supplement expenses (feed costs)
  • Allocated labour costs
  • Allocated overhead costs

And Total kg Produced = Sales kg − Purchase kg + Change in Inventory kg

Getting Started

Step 1: Select Your Enterprises

Choose which livestock enterprises you run (Cattle, Sheep, Goat). If running multiple enterprises, you'll allocate shared costs by DSE percentage.

Step 2: Trading Details

Enter your livestock reconciliation for each enterprise:

  • Opening and closing numbers for each stock class
  • Purchases and sales — numbers, weights, and prices
  • Natural increase and deaths
  • Inventory values — weight and value per head for opening and closing stock

The reconciliation must balance: Opening + Purchases + Natural Increase − Sales − Deaths = Closing

Step 3: Expenses

Enter direct expenses (herd/flock health, contractors, transport, selling costs) and supplement feed costs for each enterprise.

Step 4: Labour & Overheads

Enter total business labour and overhead costs, then allocate them across enterprises by percentage. You can allocate by DSE, labour proportion, income proportion, or custom percentages.

Step 5: Results

View your calculated COP with charts, benchmark comparisons, and the ability to export or print.

Tips

  • Use the pre-loaded case studies as examples
  • Save scenarios to compare different years or enterprises
  • The tool works offline — your data is saved locally
  • Use DSE allocation to fairly split costs between enterprises
