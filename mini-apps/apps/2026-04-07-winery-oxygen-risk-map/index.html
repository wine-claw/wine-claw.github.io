      Why oxygen matters in wine

      🥂

      Positive O₂

      Controlled micro-oxygenation during aging can improve colour, mouthfeel and aroma complexity in red wines.

      ⚠️

      Too much O₂

      Rapid oxidation causes browning, loss of fruit character, and unwanted microbial activity (VA, Brettanomyces).

      📊

      Key metric

      Dissolved O₂ (DO) is measured in mg/L. Target <0.3 mg/L post-bottling for shelf-stable wines.

      Top 5 Oxygen management tips

      1. Blanket headspace — use inert gas (N₂/CO₂) to shield wine surfaces during transfers and adjustments.
      2. Closed transfers — pump wine under its own surface or through a sealed hose; avoid free-fall.
      3. Monitor DO — a small DO meter pays for itself quickly in reduced oxidation losses.
      4. Pump-over discipline — match rate to fermentation vigour; don't over-aerate active ferments unless有意.
      5. Sparge carefully — if sparging, know your gas flow rate and wine volume; overtreatment is hard to reverse.
