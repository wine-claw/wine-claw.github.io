How it works

A sundial tells time from the Sun. The gnomon is the raised pointer; its shadow falls on marked hour lines. For a horizontal sundial the gnomon must be parallel to Earth's axis — angled up from the ground by your latitude, pointing toward the celestial pole.

tan(β) = sin(φ) · tan(H)

Here β is the hour-line angle from noon, φ is latitude, and H is the hour angle (15° per hour from noon). The shadow sweeps clockwise in the northern hemisphere and counter-clockwise in the southern.

Because Earth's orbit is slightly elliptical, sundials can drift about ±15 minutes from clock time through the year — called the equation of time. This app uses mean solar time.
